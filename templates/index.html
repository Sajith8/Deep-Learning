Soybean Seed Categories

Broken soybean

Physically damaged or split into pieces during harvesting, handling, or processing.

Imature soybeans

Seeds are not fully developed or ripened before harvesting.

Intact soybeans

Healthy, fully developed, and premium quality.

Skin-damaged soybeans

Seeds are superficial damage to their outer coat (seed coat) due to mechanical impact, pests, or disease.

About Soybean Seeds

Soybean is one of the most important crops in the world. The quality of seeds affects crop yield. Poor quality seeds can reduce production and cause losses. This classifier helps in identifying different types of soybean seeds such as healthy, broken, discolored, shriveled, and moldy — ensuring better decision-making for farmers and seed companies.

Origin of Soybean

Scientific name: Glycine max
Family: Fabaceae (the legume family)
Origin: Soybean is native to East Asia, primarily China. It has been cultivated for thousands of years—records suggest over 3,000–5,000 years ago.
Spread: From China, soybeans spread to Korea and Japan, and later to other parts of the world, including India, the USA, Brazil, and Europe.

Formation and Domestication

Wild ancestor: Soybean was derived from a wild species called Glycine soja.
Domestication traits:

  • Early farmers selected for traits like:Larger seeds (easier to harvest and more nutritious)
  • Higher yield
  • Reduced seed dispersal (so seeds stayed on the plant until harvested)

    • Over generations, selective breeding transformed the small, tough wild seeds into the cultivated soybean we know today.

    About Soybean Oil Yield


    The exact oil yield depends on:

    • Variety of soybean: Some varieties are bred for higher oil content.
    • Growing conditions: Climate, soil quality, and fertilizer usage affect oil levels.
    • Harvest maturity: Immature beans have lower oil content.
    • Processing method: Mechanical pressing extracts ~90% of oil; solvent extraction can extract up to ~99%.

    These are approximate ranges since physical damage and maturity directly impact oil yield:

    Soybean Type Expected Oil Content (% by weight) Reasoning
    Intact soybeans 18–21% (Highest) Fully mature and undamaged seeds retain maximum oil.
    Broken soybeans 17–20% Slight loss due to physical damage; oil % stays similar.
    Immature soybeans 12–16% (Lowest) Immature seeds haven’t fully developed storage lipids.
    Skin-damaged soybeans 16–19% Slightly reduced due to exposure and moisture absorption.
    Spotted soybeans 17–20% Cosmetic spots don’t drastically lower oil; slight quality drop possible.

    Key insight: Intact soybeans are ideal for oil extraction. Physical damage impacts oil quality (oxidation risk, impurities) more than percentage.
